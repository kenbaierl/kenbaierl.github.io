3D Printed Dice Tower and Holder

In my last semester at school, I took a 3D printing class that taught us how to model in Fusion 360 and use PrusaSlicer for printing on a Prusa MK3S+. The course covered the basics of printing, troubleshooting, and modeling, enabling us to create and print our own designs or use existing ones. For the final project, we had to design and print an original creation. I designed a DnD dice holder and a dice roller inspired by a tower from The Legend of Zelda: Wind Waker. Both were modeled in Fusion 360 and printed in PLA on the Prusa MK3S+. To design the dice tower in Fusion 360, I modeled a 3D rectangle and then used the coil tool to create a space for the dice to roll down the tower. One difficulty was making the slide big enough for dice to roll down so I had to measure exactly how big dice are and change the size of the slide. The prints took about 30 hours and used around 2/3 of a roll of PLA filament.
