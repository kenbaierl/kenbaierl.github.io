Line Following Robot

This project involved creating a line-following robot for a microelectronics class, utilizing only PCB components and excluding microcontrollers. The design uses two infrared sensors interfaced with inverters, triggering the motors upon detecting non-black surfaces and halting them over black tape. The robot's chassis was 3D-printed, assembled using screws and bolts to secure the battery holder and PCB. The power system connects a battery pack of 8 AA batteries connected in parallel which outputs 12 volts to a switch, activating the infrared sensors. These sensors output signals through NOT gates to N-channel MOSFETs, which drive the motors, enabling the robot to follow the line by adjusting motor activity. This robot also participated in the Chapman University Make-A-Thon engineering competition.
